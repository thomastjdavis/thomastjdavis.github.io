10/20 Warmup:

You dig through Dr. Zoo's office looking for something he lost, and you find the notes of another zoologist (Dr. Wilds) studying the relocated elephants.

You, interested, decide to read the notes.

In her notes, she writes:

"The elephant population is wildly unstable! It always seems to flucuate but not in an expected way."

Intriguged, you continue reading:

"My data indicates that the elephant population follows the function:"$$E(t) = \frac{20}{0.5+e^{-0.04t}}+e^{-0.04t}\sin(t), \quad t\geq -10,\quad \text{E is measured in thousands of elephants}$$


Can Dr. Zoo's and Dr. Wilds' models work together? Ask yourself these questions

  • Will the population of the elephants as time increases be the same as the other model?
  • Does Dr. Wilds' model invalidate Dr. Zoo's model and why?

10/15 Warmup:

Dr. Zoo travels to Africa and studies wild elephants that researchers helped relocate 45 years ago. Over time, he notices that the population follows somewhat closely to a logistic curve.

Using his data points, Dr. Zoo figures out that the elephant population follows the function $$P(t) = \frac{40}{1+2e^{-.04t}},\quad t\geq 35,\quad \text{P measured in thousands of elephants}$$


You join Dr. Zoo and he asks you, "Based on the function, what will happen to the elephant population over time? Will they eventually die out or reach a stable population?"
